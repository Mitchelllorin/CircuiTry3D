Overlay Specs
📐
Lower Third
Left edge, 10–15% from bottom. Semi-transparent dark background. 3px left-border in accent color. Slide in from left (0.3 s ease-out).
🏷️
Badge / Label
Small pill with solid accent fill, black text. Top-left. Used to label the feature mode. Always present during a scene.
📝
Headline
Large text, top-left or center. Show for 4–8 s then fade. Use text shadow: 0 2px 12px rgba(0,0,0,0.8) over busy 3D footage.
💬
Callout Box
Dark rounded card with icon + title + sub. Appears sequentially (one at a time) during failure stages. Keep under 280 px wide.
🎞️
Fade Timing
Fade in: 0.25 s. Hold. Fade out: 0.35 s. Never hard-cut overlays — always fade or slide. Overlaps look cheap.
🎨
Drop Shadow
Add text-shadow or drop-shadow on all text to ensure legibility over 3D backgrounds. Background panel behind text is safer than shadow alone.

Video Editor Tips
🎬
Lead with the explosion
For social cuts (30 s or less), open with the FUSE™ failure scene — then cut back to "this is how it starts." Pattern-interrupts the scroll.
⏱️
Keep text on screen ≥ 2 s
Even for fast-cut reels. Viewers need at least 2 seconds to read a single-line tagline at normal reading speed. 3 s is safer for two-liners.
🎵
Music hit = text hit
Time your text transitions to beat drops or musical phrase changes. The explosion callout ("💥 Catastrophic Failure") is especially powerful on a bass hit.
🔤
One message per screen
Never show more than one headline + one sub-line at the same time. Callout boxes are the exception — up to 3 in a row during the failure sequence.
🌑
Darken background if needed
If 3D footage is too bright to read text over, add a semi-transparent dark gradient behind text blocks. rgba(0,0,0,0.5) is usually enough.
