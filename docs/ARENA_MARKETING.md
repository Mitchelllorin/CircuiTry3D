## Part 2 — School Board & CTE Outreach Playbook

### Who to Approach

| Target | Why they care | Where to find them |

1. **Direct email to department heads** — personalized, references their specific program/state CTE standards

3. **Teacher referral / word of mouth** — one teacher who loves it becomes your best sales channel
4. **State CTE agency contacts** — state-level CTE directors distribute approved tool lists to all districts
5. **Perkins V grant ecosystem** — CTE programs receive federal Perkins V funding; being a curriculum-aligned tool makes you eligible for those budget line items
6. **LinkedIn outreach** — search "CTE department head", "electronics teacher", "career technical education coordinator"
7. **Twitter/X #edtech #CTEworks** — active community of CTE educators
8. **Teachers Pay Teachers / educational Facebook groups** — grass-roots teacher adoption

4. **No hardware required**: "Runs in any browser. No installation, no device requirements beyond a Chromebook or tablet."

### Grant Funding Angle

CTE programs receive federal Perkins V funding. Frame CircuiTry3D as:
- A **lab simulation tool** that extends hands-on learning beyond physical lab time
- A **safety training resource** (failure visualization = safer lab outcomes)
- A **STEM equity tool** (students without access to physical labs get the same learning experience)

These framings map to common Perkins V allowable expenditure categories.

## Part 3 — Manufacturer Product Placement

### The Concept

The Arena's **Featured Components** panel displays real manufacturer parts as pre-loaded test specimens. A student running a stress test selects "Vishay CRCW0402 100Ω" and FUSE™ runs the failure simulation against Vishay's actual datasheet values. The manufacturer name and part number are visible throughout the session.

This is **contextual, educational, and non-intrusive** — the manufacturer's part is the subject of the lesson, not an ad.

### Who to Approach

| Target | Why they care |
|---|---|
| **Component manufacturers** (Vishay, Murata, Yageo, TDK, ON Semiconductor, Infineon, Texas Instruments, STMicroelectronics) | Brand exposure to future engineers while they're learning; "first touch" brand association |
| **Component distributors** (Digi-Key, Mouser, Newark / Farnell) | Drive traffic to product pages; associate their catalog with the learning experience |
| **Electronics evaluation board makers** (Arduino, Adafruit, SparkFun) | Their target audience is exactly the students using this tool |

### How to Pitch Manufacturers

**Key message**: *"Your part is the subject of the lesson, not an interruption to it."*

**Pitch deck structure**:
1. **What the Arena does** — show the FUSE™ simulation running on a generic resistor
2. **The placement opportunity** — show the Featured Components panel with a real card
3. **What students see** — manufacturer name, part number, specs, and the FUSE™ simulation running against their datasheet
4. **Who sees it** — CTE students, electronics students, educators, hobbyists actively stress-testing components
5. **What we need from you** — part number, key datasheet parameters, your product page URL, and logo

**Outreach channels for manufacturers**:
- **LinkedIn** — search "component marketing manager", "product manager passive components", "distributor relations"
- **Direct email to marketing teams** — most major manufacturers list press/marketing contacts publicly
- **Distribution channel** — approach Digi-Key / Mouser first; they have relationships with all major manufacturers

```

[Your name]
CircuiTry3D
hello@circuitry3d.com
```

### Technical Implementation

Manufacturer partnerships translate directly into entries in the `FEATURED_COMPONENTS` array in `public/arena.html`. Each entry specifies:

```js
{
  id: "vendor-partnumber",          // unique ID
  mfr: "Manufacturer Name",         // displayed on the card
  name: "Part Number",              // displayed on the card
  spec: "key spec summary",         // one-line spec display
  type: "resistor",                 // FailureEngine family key
  props: {                          // datasheet values for FUSE™
    resistance: 100,
    powerRating: 0.063,
    tolerance: 0.01,
    thermalResistance: 300
  }
}
```

This requires only a few lines of JSON per component — the FUSE™ simulation handles the rest automatically.
